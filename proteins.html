Proteins

Building blocks for your muscles

Your Protein needs

Proteins are made up of chains of smaller chemicals called amino acids. The human body cannot store amino acids, so it must be supplied daily from the foods we eat.

Protein makes up the building blocks of organs, muscles, skin, hormones and pretty much everything that matters in your body. for this reason, you should eat high-quality protein at every meal.

The amount of protein you need in your diet depends on your weight, age and health. As a rough guide

  • 0.75 g/kg for adult women
  • 0.84 g/kg for adult men
  • Around 1 g/kg for pregnant and breastfeeding women, and for men and women over 70 years.

SOURCES OF PROTEINS:

  • lean meat, poultry
  • fish and eggs
  • dairy products like milk, yoghurt and cheese
  • seeds and nuts
  • beans and legumes (such as lentils and chickpeas)
  • soy products like tofu
  • some grain and cereal-based products are also sources of protein, but are generally not as high in protein as meat and meat alternative products.

    • Very high protein diets combined with very low carbohydrate intakes are not recommended.
